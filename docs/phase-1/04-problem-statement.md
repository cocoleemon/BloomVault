# 🌸 Problem Statement

> *"Beauty research has never been easier to access, yet it has never been harder to organize."*

---

# Introduction

Today's beauty enthusiasts have access to more information than ever before.

Product reviews, ingredient databases, dermatologist advice, social media recommendations, online communities, and beauty influencers have made researching products more accessible and educational.

However, while information is abundant, it is also fragmented.

Users constantly switch between multiple apps and platforms to gather information, making the research process overwhelming and difficult to manage.

BloomVault exists because beauty research deserves a dedicated home.

---

# The Core Problem

There is currently no single platform that allows beauty enthusiasts to research, organize, compare, and remember beauty products throughout their decision-making journey.

Instead, users rely on disconnected tools that were never designed to work together.

As a result, valuable research becomes scattered, forgotten, and difficult to revisit.

---

# Key Problems

## 1. Beauty Research Is Scattered

Research often happens across many different platforms.

A typical journey may include:

* TikTok for product discovery
* Reddit for community experiences
* YouTube for in-depth reviews
* Ingredient databases for formulation research
* Shopping apps for pricing
* Notes apps for reminders
* Screenshots for later reference

Although each platform serves a purpose, none of them provide a complete picture.

Users become responsible for connecting all of the information themselves.

---

## 2. Users Lose the Context Behind Saved Products

Saving a product is easy.

Remembering *why* it was saved is much harder.

Over time, wishlists become long collections of products with no explanation.

Users often forget:

* What caught their attention
* Which skin concern the product addressed
* Who recommended it
* Which product it was being compared against
* Whether it was suitable for their skin type

Without context, saved products lose much of their value.

---

## 3. Product Comparison Requires Too Much Manual Effort

Choosing between similar products often requires opening multiple browser tabs and comparing information manually.

Users frequently compare:

* Ingredients
* Active concentrations
* Price
* Product size
* Skin type suitability
* Texture
* Brand reputation

This process is time-consuming and mentally demanding.

---

## 4. Ingredient Information Can Be Difficult to Understand

Ingredient names are often scientific, unfamiliar, and intimidating.

Many users want answers to practical questions, such as:

* What does this ingredient do?
* Is it suitable for sensitive skin?
* Can it be used with retinoids?
* Is it safe during the day?
* Who should avoid it?

Reliable information exists, but it is rarely presented in a way that feels approachable for everyday users.

---

## 5. Existing Beauty Apps Focus on Individual Tasks

Many current applications excel at one specific function.

Some focus on shopping.

Others focus on ingredient education.

Others focus on routine tracking.

Others emphasize social engagement.

Very few combine these experiences into one cohesive, personal workspace.

Users are left switching between multiple applications to complete a single research journey.

---

## 6. Personal Beauty Research Is Difficult to Preserve

Beauty research evolves over time.

Users discover new products, learn more about ingredients, change routines, and revisit previous decisions.

Unfortunately, this knowledge is often stored in temporary places such as screenshots, bookmarks, or notes.

There is no dedicated system for preserving personal beauty research in an organized and searchable way.

---

# Impact on Users

These challenges create unnecessary friction throughout the beauty journey.

Users may experience:

* Decision fatigue when choosing between products.
* Repeated research on products they have already investigated.
* Forgotten recommendations and notes.
* Confusing or cluttered wishlists.
* Difficulty understanding ingredients.
* Reduced confidence before making a purchase.
* Information overload from too many disconnected sources.

Instead of enjoying the research process, users spend time managing information.

---

# The Opportunity

Beauty consumers are becoming increasingly intentional.

Rather than purchasing products impulsively, many users now invest time in learning, comparing, and understanding what works for them.

This shift creates an opportunity for a new type of product.

Not another shopping application.

Not another ingredient database.

Not another routine tracker.

A personal beauty library.

BloomVault brings together research, organization, education, and thoughtful decision-making into one beautifully designed experience.

By helping users remember not only **what** they saved, but also **why** they saved it, BloomVault transforms beauty research into a lasting personal knowledge library.

---

# Problem Statement Summary

The beauty industry offers endless information, but very little organization.

BloomVault addresses this gap by providing a dedicated space where users can discover, research, compare, organize, and remember beauty products throughout their personal beauty journey.

Instead of fragmented research spread across multiple platforms, BloomVault creates a single source of truth for every beauty enthusiast.
