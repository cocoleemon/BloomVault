# 🌸 Glossary

> *"A shared language creates a shared understanding."*

---

# Introduction

This glossary defines the terminology used throughout the BloomVault project.

Its purpose is to maintain consistency across product documentation, design, engineering, and future development.

Whenever a new feature or document is created, the terms defined here should be used consistently.

---

# Product Terms

## BloomVault

The official name of the product.

**Definition**

A research-first mobile application that serves as a personal beauty library where users can discover, organize, compare, and understand beauty products.

---

## Beauty Library

The heart of BloomVault.

**Definition**

A personalized collection of beauty products saved by the user.

The Beauty Library is the primary experience of the app and represents the user's beauty knowledge, discoveries, and collections.

---

## Saved Product

A product intentionally added to a user's Beauty Library.

A saved product may include:

* Personal notes
* Collections
* Tags
* Purchase status
* Routine placement
* Personal ratings
* Comparison history

---

## Collection

A custom group of products created by the user.

Examples:

* Summer Skincare
* Korean Beauty
* Future Purchases
* Acne Care
* Holy Grails

Collections help organize the Beauty Library.

---

## Wishlist

A collection of products the user is interested in purchasing in the future.

Unlike a traditional shopping wishlist, products may remain here for research purposes even if they are never purchased.

---

## Routine

A personalized sequence of products organized by the user.

Examples:

* Morning Routine
* Evening Routine
* Travel Routine

---

## Ingredient

A cosmetic ingredient contained within a beauty product.

Each ingredient may include educational information such as:

* Purpose
* Benefits
* Skin compatibility
* Usage considerations
* Related ingredients

---

## Product Comparison

A feature that allows users to evaluate two or more products side by side.

Comparisons may include:

* Ingredients
* Benefits
* Skin suitability
* Product details
* Personal notes

---

## Beauty Journal

A personal space where users document experiences, reflections, and observations throughout their beauty journey.

This feature is planned for a future release.

---

# User Terms

## User

A registered BloomVault account holder.

---

## Beauty Researcher

BloomVault's primary user type.

A person who enjoys researching beauty products before making purchasing decisions.

Beauty Researchers value learning, organization, and thoughtful decision-making.

---

## Personal Notes

Private notes attached to products by the user.

Notes help preserve the context behind each saved product.

---

# Product Principles

## Research-First

BloomVault's guiding philosophy.

Research, education, and understanding always come before purchasing.

---

## Personal Library

The idea that BloomVault belongs to the user.

Every collection, routine, note, and saved product contributes to a unique and personal beauty library.

---

## Context

The reason a product was saved.

Context may include:

* Personal thoughts
* Recommendations
* Intended skin concern
* Comparison notes
* Purchase intentions

BloomVault values preserving context as much as preserving products.

---

# Technical Terms

## MVP

**Minimum Viable Product**

The first public version of BloomVault containing the essential features needed to solve the core user problem.

---

## React Native

The mobile framework used to develop BloomVault for both iOS and Android.

---

## Expo

The development platform used to simplify React Native development, testing, and deployment.

---

## Supabase

The backend platform powering authentication, database, storage, and backend services.

---

## PostgreSQL

The relational database used to store BloomVault's application data.

---

# Design Terms

## Product Card

A visual component representing a beauty product.

Typically displays:

* Product image
* Brand
* Product name
* Category
* Status
* Quick actions

---

## Empty State

A screen shown when no content is available.

Example:

A new user's Beauty Library before saving their first product.

Every empty state should encourage exploration rather than feel unfinished.

---

## User Flow

The sequence of steps a user takes to complete a task.

Example:

Discover Product → Save Product → Add Note → Organize into Collection
